4. Step-by-Step Wiring Guide

A. Prepare the Perfboard

  1. Plan layout: place the XIAO on one side, BNO085 on the other, battery underneath or to the side. A 5×7 cm board fits everything comfortably.
  2. Solder two rows of female pin headers (1×7) for the XIAO. This lets you pull the XIAO out for USB flashing without desoldering.
  3. Solder female pin headers for the BNO085 breakout (1×9 or 1×7 depending on your board version). Orient it so SDA/SCL/VIN/GND face the XIAO side.

B. Power Connections

  1. Solder the SPST slide switch onto the perfboard near the XIAO's bottom edge.
  2. Run a wire from the LiPo's positive terminal (or JST connector pin) to one leg of the switch.
  3. Run a wire from the other leg of the switch to the XIAO's BAT+ pad (bottom of the board — you may need to solder from underneath).
  4. Run a wire from the LiPo's negative terminal directly to the XIAO's BAT− pad.
Built-in charging: When USB-C is plugged in, the XIAO ESP32C3 automatically charges the LiPo through its onboard charge IC. No external TP4056 needed. The charge current is ~50mA (safe for cells ≥200mAh).
LiPo safety: Only use cells with a built-in protection circuit (most pouch cells from reputable suppliers include one). Verify polarity with a multimeter before connecting. Reversing polarity will damage the XIAO.

C. I2C Connections (XIAO → BNO085)

  1. Wire 3V3 (XIAO right side) → VIN (BNO085). The Adafruit BNO085 breakout has an onboard regulator and level shifter, so 3.3V input is fine.
  2. Wire GND (XIAO right side) → GND (BNO085).
  3. Wire D4 / GPIO6 (XIAO left side) → SDA (BNO085).
  4. Wire D5 / GPIO7 (XIAO left side) → SCL (BNO085).
  5. Solder the 100nF ceramic capacitor between VIN and GND on the BNO085 side, as close to the breakout as possible. This is a decoupling cap to prevent I2C noise.
No external pull-ups needed. The Adafruit BNO085 breakout includes 10kΩ pull-up resistors on SDA and SCL.

D. Pre-Power Checklist

  1. Continuity check: Use multimeter to verify 3V3→VIN and GND→GND paths have continuity.
  2. Short check: Verify NO continuity between 3V3 and GND (no shorts).
  3. Battery polarity: Measure LiPo voltage with multimeter. Confirm (+) goes to switch → BAT+ and (−) goes to BAT−.
  4. Visual inspection: Check for solder bridges, especially around the fine-pitch XIAO pads.

5. Master vs Slave — No Hardware Difference

All 3 boards are wired identically. The role (master or slave) is determined entirely by which firmware is flashed:

Master

Flash master/ firmware. Connect to PC via USB-C for serial bridge. The master can also run on battery (untethered) if you add a wireless backhaul later.

Slave ×2

Flash slave/ firmware. Runs on LiPo battery, completely wireless. Node ID auto-assigned from MAC address — no configuration needed.
